# Getting Started with Advocacy

Welcome to the advocacy team at Microsoft/GitHub! This guide will help you get oriented quickly and start making an impact.

## What Is Developer Advocacy?

Developer advocacy is the practice of championing products, tools, and platforms to developer communities. Advocates act as a bridge between the engineering teams who build products and the developers who use them. The goal is to:

- Help developers succeed with the tools and products your team builds.
- Surface real developer feedback back to product and engineering teams.
- Grow awareness, adoption, and engagement across developer audiences.

## Your First 30 Days

### Week 1 – Learn the Product

- Get access to all internal documentation, repositories, and communication channels.
- Set up and use the product you are advocating for end-to-end.
- Identify the top 3–5 scenarios your target developer audience cares about most.
- Connect with the product and engineering teams.

### Week 2 – Know the Community

- Identify the key communities, forums, and events where your target audience is active.
- Review existing samples, demos, blog posts, and videos that already exist for the product.
- Follow relevant accounts, hashtags, and newsletters.

### Week 3 – Create Something

- Build a small sample or demo that covers a scenario relevant to your audience.
- Write a short blog post, tweet thread, or LinkedIn post about it.
- Share your content with your team for feedback before publishing.

### Week 4 – Engage

- Publish your content across relevant [channels](channels.md).
- Engage with comments and responses from the community.
- Capture any feedback or feature requests you hear and share them with product teams.

## Core Responsibilities

| Area | What You Do |
|---|---|
| Content creation | Blogs, videos, samples, demos, tutorials |
| Community engagement | Forums, social media, Q&A, events |
| Event presence | Conferences, meetups, hackathons |
| Internal feedback loop | Share developer sentiment with product teams |
| Measurement | Track reach, engagement, and adoption metrics |

## Key Tools

- **GitHub** – For hosting and sharing samples, tracking issues, and contributing to open source.
- **Microsoft Learn** – For publishing tutorials and learning paths.
- **Dev.to, Medium, or Tech Community** – For blog posts.
- **LinkedIn, X (Twitter), Mastodon** – For social media promotion.
- **YouTube** – For video content and demo recordings.
