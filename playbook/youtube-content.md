# YouTube Content

Video is engaging, discoverable long after publication, and can be embedded in blogs and courses. There are a few paths to getting content on YouTube.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Impact Level** | High |
| **Lead Time** | 2-4 weeks |
| **Effort Required** | Medium-High |
| **Best For** | Visual demos, tutorials, reaching new audiences |

## Microsoft YouTube Channels

| Channel | What's On It |
|---------|-------------|
| [Microsoft Reactor](https://www.youtube.com/@microsoftreactor) | Livestreams, workshops, Model Mondays, community events |
| [Visual Studio Code](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCs5Y5_7XK8HLDX0SLNwkd3w) | VS Code tutorials, features, extensions (e.g. Gwen's DocumentDB intro series) |
| Product-specific channels | Check if your product area has one |

## Three Paths to YouTube

1. **Reactor Livestream** — schedule a livestream, it's automatically recorded and published. Lowest effort path to a YouTube video. See [Reactor Livestreams](./reactor-livestreams.md).
2. **Partner channel** — coordinate with a relevant Microsoft YouTube channel to record content (pre-recorded or live).
3. **For Beginners videos** — create video content as part of a course; videos are embedded in lessons.

## Content Types

- **Livestream recordings** — do a livestream, get a video
- **Pre-recorded tutorials** — more polished, can be edited
- **Quick tips/shorts** — brief, focused content for specific features
- **Series** — multi-part content, works well with For Beginners courses

## Works Well With

- **Blogs** — embed videos in blog posts
- **For Beginners** — videos become lesson content
- **Reactor** — livestreams become YouTube videos
- **Newsletters** — link to new videos

## Tips

- Test your demo thoroughly before recording
- Pin dependency versions
- Have backup content (pre-recorded segments or screenshots)
- Include links in video descriptions
- Write a companion blog post for extra reach
