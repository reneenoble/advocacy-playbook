# Conference Sponsored Sessions

When Microsoft sponsors a conference (PyCon, JSConf, etc.), the sponsorship package often includes speaking slots. These are **limited and managed through advocacy** — you can't just sign up, but you can express interest early.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Impact Level** | High |
| **Lead Time** | 3-6 months |
| **Effort Required** | Medium-High |
| **Best For** | Community credibility, reaching specific developer communities |

## How It Works

- Work through advocacy — they manage sponsorship relationships
- Express interest early when planning begins
- Slots are allocated among interested teams
- This isn't something you can just choose — think of it as something to be invited into

## Other Conference Opportunities

If sponsored slots aren't available, there are still options:

- **Smaller conferences** aligned with your product area
- **Regional events** and local meetups
- **Community partnerships** and user groups
- Look out for sponsorship opportunities at other conferences that align with your product — you could sponsor those directly and get speaking slots

## Content Reuse

Conference content feeds nicely into other channels:
- Polished demos → YouTube content
- Session material → blog posts or course content
- Conference recordings → evergreen video
